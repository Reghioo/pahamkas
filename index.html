WHAT IS PAHAMKAS?

The group's brand name, "PAHAMKAS," was created with the meaning of "PAHAM," an old Filipino term and a derived word from the Sanskrit language that denotes a scholarly person. The word "KAS" came from a podcast.

MISION

The team is committed to promoting education, community, and resilience. Through collaboration, education, and assistance, we work to empower students and build a better future for all.

VISION

The team serves as a gateway to limitless potential. In order to empower people to have a positive impact on the world, we work to build communities that value education, encourage curiosity, and support learning.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What are the benefits of streaming technology over more conventional content delivery techniques, and how does it work?

    Streaming is more efficient than downloading media files because it is real-time. When a video file is downloaded, a copy of the complete file is saved to the hard drive of the device, and the video cannot be played until the entire file is downloaded.

  • What problems with user experience and content delivery do streaming services encounter, and how are these problems solved?

    Buffering, poor video quality, and content discovery concerns plague streaming services. Adaptive streaming, tailored recommendations, consistent user interfaces, accessibility features, enhanced content discovery algorithms, and rigorous security measures are among the solutions available to ensure a seamless and secure user experience.

  • What is the empathy phase in the design thinking process?

    During the empathy phase, the group spends time getting to know the user and understanding their needs, desires, and objectives. This entails seeing and engaging with individuals in order to comprehend them on a psychological and emotional level. You must put aside your assumptions at the empathize phase.

  • What role does empathy play in understanding the needs and difficulties of the audience during the design thinking process?

    Empathy is the first stage in design thinking since it is a skill that allows us to comprehend and share the same feelings as others. Empathy allows us to put ourselves in the shoes of another and understand how they may be feeling about their problem, scenario, or situation.

  • What are the common challenges faced during audience analysis, and how can they be overcome?

    The lack of solid and relevant data is one of the most difficult issues of audience analysis. Addressing obstacles such as limited or skewed data, shifting demographics, potential misinterpretation, privacy concerns are all part of navigating audience analysis challenges.

  • How can empathy mapping be used effectively to understand the emotions and experiences of the audience?

    Empathy mapping enables us to better understand their target audience by taking into account their perspectives, desires, and pain points. It contributes to gaining deeper insights into customers' behaviors, emotions, and goals, resulting in more informed decision-making and successful communication techniques.

  • How do you analyze and connect the information collected from the audience analysis?

    To properly analyze audience data, begin by acquiring a variety of information through surveys, social media, and feedback. Sort and analyze this data, looking for trends and recurring patterns. To comprehend experiences, create rich audience personas and map consumer journeys. For validation, incorporate direct feedback and cross-reference data. Recognize and translate significant patterns and behaviors into actionable insights. Monitor and adjust plans on a regular basis in response to changing audience preferences and trends.
