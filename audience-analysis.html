BELIEFS AND OPINIONS

Facebook Application Analysis:
Frequency of Facebook Usage During the Pandemic:

Respondents were asked to indicate how "frequently" they utilized Facebook during the pandemic by employing a scale-based question:

  • 10.4% (5 respondents) selected "never" or "1" on the scale.

  • 18.8% (9 respondents) selected "rarely" or "2" on the scale.

  • 20.8% (10 respondents) selected "sometimes" or "3" on the scale.

  • 16.7% (8 respondents) selected "often" or "4" on the scale.

  • 33.3% (16 respondents) selected "always" or "5" on the scale.

Respondents were asked to indicate how "long" they utilized Facebook during the pandemic:

  • 58.3% (28 respondents) selected “30 Minutes - 1 hour”

  • 12.5% (6 respondents) selected "1 hour - 2 hours”

  • 10.4% (5 respondents) selected "2 hours - 4 hours”

  • 6.3% (3 respondents) selected “4 hours - 6 hours”

  • 4.2% (2 respondents) selected "6 hours - 8 hours”

  • 8.3% (4 respondents) selected "8 hours above”

Impact to Respondents Academic Performance:
Academic Performance Impact:

Here respondents identify whether or not Facebook usage positively or negatively impact their academic performance.

  • 62.5% (30 respondents) believe that Facebook usage positively impacted their academic performance.

  • 37.5% (18 respondents) believe that Facebook usage does not positively impact their academic performance.

Academic Connection Impact Among Peers:

Here respondents identify the academic connection impact among peers whether or not it has a connection.

  • 77.1% (37 respondents) believe that Facebook usage affects their academic connection among peers.

  • 22.9% (11 respondents) believe that Facebook does not affect their academic connection among peers.

Impact on Current Social Media Usage After the Pandemic:

Here respondents identify the impact on current social media usage after the pandemic whether or not it has a positive or negative impact.

  • 54.2% (26 respondents) believe that Facebook usage during the pandemic affected their current social media usage after the pandemic.

  • 45.8% (22 respondents) believe that Facebook usage during the pandemic does not affect their current social media usage after the pandemic.

Perspectives on Pandemic Facebook Usage in Balancing Act Between Academics and Social Interactions:

Here are the perspectives of the respondents regarding how using Facebook during the pandemic presented challenges in balancing their academic responsibilities and social interactions:

  • 41% (20 respondents) believe it is 'neutral'.

  • 22.9% (11 respondents) believe it is 'somewhat challenging'.

  • 12.5% (6 respondents) believe it is 'very challenging'.

  • 12.5% (6 respondents) believe it is 'not challenging'.

  • 10.4% (5 respondents) believe it is 'not applicable' because they don’t use Facebook.

CHARACTERISTICS OF A GOOD STREAM

In the current digital age, students have a variety of opinions about how social media impacts their academic performance. While some see it as an advantageous educational tool, others see it as a source of distraction. This delicate balance is highlighted by the opinions offered by our respondents, who presented a wide range of viewpoints. Our investigation is built on this diversity of viewpoints, which offers insightful information about the various beliefs regarding the impact of social media on students' academic success below is the summary of our respondents:

  • Diverse Perspectives - Respondents' opinions on how social media affects academic performance are diverse, ranging from their view of it as a useful educational tool to their expression of worries about distractions and diminished focus.

  • Positive aspects -Some respondents value social media's contribution to knowledge dissemination, research assistance, student connection, and accessibility to educational content, including videos and live-streaming lectures.

  • Negative Concerns - Social media distractions and procrastination are a source of concern. Some respondents draw attention to the addictive nature of social media, which could have an adverse impact on mental health and academic performance.

  • Balancing Act - The complexity of the relationship between social media and academic performance is emphasized. While it can enhance learning through educational content and collaborative opportunities, it can also lead to decreased motivation and concentration, affecting overall academic performance.

  • Importance of Moderation - Many respondents stressed the need for responsible usage and self-discipline. They highlight that the impact on academic performance varies significantly based on individual time management and self-control when using social media platforms.

  • Challenges in Time Management - The challenge of balancing social media use with academic responsibilities, including projects and exams, is acknowledged. Social media's potential to interfere with effective time management and focus is a shared concern among respondents.

  • Educational Potential - Despite concerns, some respondents recognize social media's potential to enhance learning experiences, particularly through access to educational resources, online study groups, and informative content.

  • Complex Dynamics -The attitudes showcased reveal a delicate balance between recognizing social media's educational benefits and acknowledging its potential distractions. The relationship between social media utilization and academic performance is complex and multifaceted, shaped by individual behaviors and attitudes.
